When I Hear "Accessibility"

When I hear the word "accessibility," I think of fairness, empathy, and opportunity. Accessibility means ensuring that everyone—regardless of ability, device, or background— can access and enjoy digital content. On the web, accessibility isn't a bonus feature; it's a basic human right. I believe the internet should be inclusive, not exclusive.

Automated Tools are NOT Enough

Automated tools are great for catching common accessibility issues, but they are far from complete. These tools can't tell if the alt text is meaningful, if the focus order makes sense, or if the content structure is logical for assistive technologies. Manual testing, user feedback, and empathy are essential for achieving true accessibility.

Accessibility Standards I Use

I follow the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) 2.1 to ensure my projects are perceivable, operable, understandable, and robust. I use semantic HTML, descriptive alt text, proper color contrast, and ARIA attributes responsibly. I also test with screen readers and validate accessibility using multiple tools.

Why Inclusion During Design Matters

Including people with disabilities during the design process helps identify real-world barriers early on. Inclusive design means considering different abilities, ages, and experiences to build something better for everyone.

My Accessibility Commitment

I am committed to ensuring digital accessibility for people with disabilities. I continually improve the user experience for everyone and apply relevant accessibility standards. My goal is to conform to WCAG 2.1 Level AA guidelines. If you encounter any accessibility barriers in my work, please contact me.

Conformance status: My websites are partially conformant with WCAG 2.1 level AA, meaning some content may not fully meet all accessibility guidelines. I am actively working to improve this.

Feedback: I welcome your feedback on the accessibility of my websites. Please let me know if you encounter accessibility barriers.

Technical specifications: Accessibility relies on HTML, CSS, JavaScript, and WAI-ARIA following WCAG guidelines.
